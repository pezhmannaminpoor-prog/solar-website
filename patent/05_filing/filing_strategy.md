# Filing Strategy

## Routes
- Provisional (US): establish priority; informal; no claims required; 12-month clock
- Non-provisional (US utility): full examination; claims required
- PCT (international phase): defers national costs ~30 months; international search/opinion
- Direct national filings: EU (EPO), CN, JP, etc.

## Decision points
- Urgency: public disclosure/launch date?
- Maturity: data and drawings ready?
- Budget: provisional now vs direct non-provisional vs PCT
- Markets: where to protect and enforce

## Typical timeline
- Day 0: file provisional (or NP if ready)
- Month 12: convert to NP and/or file PCT
- Month 16: publication
- Month 30/31: national phase entries

## Cost ranges (very rough)
- Provisional: $1k–$4k DIY; $4k–$10k with counsel
- US non-provisional: $8k–$20k+ drafting; prosecution $3k–$10k+
- PCT: $5k–$12k filing; national phase $10k–$40k+/country over life

## Documentation to prepare
- Spec with enabling detail, drawings, and claims (if NP)
- IDS (information disclosure statement) with prior-art list (US)
- Assignments, inventor declarations

## Risk controls
- Avoid public disclosure pre-filing; use NDAs
- Mark as “Patent Pending” after filing
